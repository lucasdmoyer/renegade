Volatility Smiles How options are priced in the real world

One of the main assumptions of the black-sholes model is that the returns of the underlying asset is log-normally distributed, but what happens when the returns are not log-normal?

Volatility is one of the main ingredients used in the black-sholes model to price options, and volatility depends on the option's strike price and time to maturity. So how can we use real world (implied) volatilities to price our options?
