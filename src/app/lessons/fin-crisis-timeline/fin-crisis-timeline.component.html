2008 Financial Crisis Timeline

Start-2007

Growing concerns

June 8th

Amid growing concern about the housing market's reliance on subprime loans (often with those on poor credit), the DIJI plunge 198.94 points or 1.5% to 13266.73. Treasuries see some of their sharpest losses in years, pushing the yield on 10-year notes up to 5.10% amid simmering inflation jitters.

U.S. Bond Yield hits 5-year high

June 13th

U.S. bond yields hit a five-year high as investors continue to sell Treasuries, with the yield on the benchmark 10-year note rising to 5.249%

Financial Industry takes a hit

June 14th

Bear Stearns reports a 10% decline in quarterly earnings as the mortgage market shows signs of cracking. Chief Financial Officer Sam Molinaro says, "We are impacted in a weaker mortgage market until that industry turns around".

Further damage

June 18th

Reports say Merrill Lynch seized collateral from a Bear Stearns hedge fund invested heavily in subprime loans

Hedge fund trouble

June 22nd

Bear Stearns commits $3.2 billion in secured loans to bail out its High-Grade Structured Credit Fund, says company's troubles are "relatively contained".

Blackstone Group's inital public offering is priced at $31 a share, raising as much as $4.6 billion; the next day, the stock jumps 13%.

Further trouble

June 23rd

Bear Stearns agrees to lend as much as $3.2 billion to one of its two troubled hedge funds.

Federal-funds rate remains unchanged at 5.25%

June 28th

Federal Reserve policy makers, on the second day of a two-day meeting, announce the key federal-funds rate will remain unchanged at 5.25%.

BCE Inc. Buyout

July 2nd

BCE Inc. agrees to be acquired by the investment arm of ONtario Teachers' Pension Plan, Providence Equity Partners Inc. and Madison Dearbon Partners for $32.6 billion. It is the largest private-equity buyout in history.

UBS leadership change

July 9th

After a series of setbacks, including the collapse of an internal hedge fund, Swiss financial firm UBS removes Chief Executive Peter Wuffli, Marcel Rohner, the deputy CEO and head of private-banking and wealth-management operations, succeeds him.

DIJA surge

July 13th

The DIJA surges 283.86 points or 2.1%, to a record 13861.73, its biggest gain in nearly four years.

Hold 'ma beer

July 14th

Oil climbs to almost $74 a barrel. The DIJA hit a record for a second straight session, rising 45.52 points to 13907.52 despite a jump in oil prices.

Bear problems

July 17

Bear Stearns tells clients that the assets in one of its troubled funds are essentially worthless, while those in the other are worth 9% of what their value was at the end of April.
