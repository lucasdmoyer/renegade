2008 Financial Crisis Timeline

Start, 2007

Growing concerns

June 8th

Amid growing concern about the housing market's reliance on subprime loans (often with those on poor credit), the DIJI plunge 198.94 points or 1.5% to 13266.73. Treasuries see some of their sharpest losses in years, pushing the yield on 10-year notes up to 5.10% amid simmering inflation jitters.

U.S. Bond Yield hits 5-year high

June 13th

U.S. bond yields hit a five-year high as investors continue to sell Treasuries, with the yield on the benchmark 10-year note rising to 5.249%

Financial Industry takes a hit

June 14th

Bear Stearns reports a 10% decline in quarterly earnings as the mortgage market shows signs of cracking. Chief Financial Officer Sam Molinaro says, "We are impacted in a weaker mortgage market until that industry turns around".

Further damage

June 18th

Reports say Merrill Lynch seized collateral from a Bear Stearns hedge fund invested heavily in subprime loans

Hedge fund trouble

June 22nd

Bear Stearns commits $3.2 billion in secured loans to bail out its High-Grade Structured Credit Fund, says company's troubles are "relatively contained".

Blackstone Group's inital public offering is priced at $31 a share, raising as much as $4.6 billion; the next day, the stock jumps 13%.

Further trouble

June 23rd

Bear Stearns agrees to lend as much as $3.2 billion to one of its two troubled hedge funds.
