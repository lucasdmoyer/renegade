Other terms

Consumption vs Investment Assets

Investment assets are assets held by significant numbers of people purely for investment purposes (stocks, bonds)

Consumption assets are assets help primarily for consumption (oil, copper)

Some assets can be both. It depends on the intention of the owner.

Short Selling

Short selling involves selling securities you do not own.

Since they are selling an asset they don't own, they borrow one from another party. If that asset makes any income over the period of the borrowing, the short seller must compensate the lender with payment (coupon, etc).

With the borrowed asset, the short seller can sell it, but they must return the same asset to the borrower at maturity.

A short seller is bearish on asset since they hope they can buy back the asset at a lower price.

Assumptions

  • No transaction costs
  • Same tax rate on all net trading profits
  • Market participants can borrow and/or lend money at the same risk free rate
  • Market participants take advantage of any existing arbitrage opportunity.
